Gentle Grasping: A Universal Method with Low-Cost Magnetic Tactile Sensors

Yi Liu*, Remko Proesmans, Andreas Verleysen, Francis wyffels
IDLab-AIRO, elis, Ghent University - imec
*Corresponding Author
Jun 04, 2024

Abstract

This paper presents a tactile-based grasping scheme with a two-fingered parallel gripper equipped with four low-cost magnetic tactile sensors. The tactile sensor provides three-dimensional information during grasping. Initially, we utilize the magnetic tactile sensors for slip detection during the lifting stage. Furthermore, we propose a force approximation method that uses dynamic force increments to find the object's critical value of slip. The robot can grasp objects with minimal force in this way. To validate the effectiveness of the proposed method, we conducted tests on 20 different everyday objects. The results show that the selected slip detection metric can be deployed on low-cost magnetic tactile sensors and the force approximation method based on dynamic force increments can quickly obtain the critical value of slip for common objects.
